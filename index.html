4 Phases of Project Management

Planning

Determine the viability of the investment and obtain an agreement to pay, and plan the control of the project.

Execution

Plan the specifics of the project, and perform all activities to complete a successful project.

Implementation

Go live with the project work product.

Closure

Complete the documentation to close out the project.

Business Needs

Every business entity looks to attain three things:

  1. Achieve objective.
  2. Manage risks.
  3. Have fully utilized resources.
Objectives
  • Objectives are NOT wishes.
  • Must have well-defined outcome expectations.
  • Must be achievable.
  • Should have some initial plan.
Risks
  • Risk of not achieving objectives.
  • Environmental risk to employees.
  • Business created a risk to the outside environment.
  • Risk of foul perception.
  • Other Risks?
Fully Engaged Resources

Concept of Value

Circumstances
Understand the customer and his circumstances, what he expects.
Needs or wants
Know what the customer wants or needs.
Perception
  1. Value is defined by the customer.
  2. Affordable mix of features.
  3. Achievement of objectives.
  4. Value changes over time and circumstances.

Utility

  • Fit for purpose (works as designed).
  • Improves probability of achieving outcomes.
  • Improves customer performance capability.
  • Reduces customer constraints.
